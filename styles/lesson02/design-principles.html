Visual Hierarchy

Organization/Company's Name: Apple Inc.

Apple's website is well organized. They use different sizes, colors, and spaces to help you see the important tech first. When you look at it on your phone, you'll notice that pictures, big titles, and buttons are all arranged so you know where to look first. It's a map for your eyes, making sure you don't miss anything important.

Hick's Law

Organization/Company's Name: Amazon

Amazon's mobile interface applies Hick's Law effectively by simplifying the decision-making process. The homepage prominently displays categories, recent searches, and personalized recommendations, reducing the cognitive load for users and enabling quicker navigation to desired products.

Fitt's Law

Organization/Company's Name: Google

Google's search interface is a great example of Fitt's Law in action. The search bar, a primary interactive element, is large and centrally positioned, making it easy for users to target and interact with. This design choice aligns with Fitt's Law, optimizing the speed and accuracy of user interactions. It's a simple, yet effective design make google a great option for your search engine.
